ESN Germany e.V.

The Erasmus Student Network Germany, a non-profit student organisation, is the consolidation of the German local Erasmus student initiatives and their volunteers. Our voluntary members, mostly students, take care of approximately 10,600 exchange students every year in about 40 sections all over Germany. In Europe, ESN is represented in more than 1,000 Higher Education Institutions from 42 countries. This makes our organisation one of the largest student initiatives in Europe.

The National Board

ESN Germany is formed by the National Board. Its purpose is to support the local sections in all kinds of topics, to be an intermediate between them and ESN International, and to strengthen the German network plus the relationships between the sections and their members. Each one of the board members of ESN Germany is a volunteer and was elected at our January National Assembly by our sections.

The Audit Board

The Audit Board is formed by the Main Chair, the Finance Audit and the Legal Audit. Their tasks are among other things to audit general processes, monitor the National Board’s work, watch over the adherence to our SOS, promote compliance and check the budget.

The Alumni

The ESN Alumni Deutschland e.V. is an association of both former and current ESN members. They have been - and still are - a big support for all active members by sharing their experiences and knowledge as well as offering financial support when needed. Since its foundation 2010 the alumni association has grown continuously and is now one of the most active within ESN.

ESN International

Erasmus Student Network (ESN) is the biggest student association in Europe. It was born on the 16th October 1989 and legally registered in 1990 for supporting and developing student exchange. We are present in more than 1000 Higher Education Institutions from 42 countries. The network is constantly developing and expanding. We have around 15,000 active members that are in many sections supported by so-called buddies mainly taking care of international students. Thus, ESN involves around 50,000 young people offering its services to around 350,000 international students every year.
